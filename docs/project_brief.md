# Project Brief: Regional Infrastructure Resilience Auditor

## Objective
Build a decision-support analytics product that standardizes childcare, youth welfare, and hospital infrastructure statistics across German regions and years to identify underserved areas, quantify service maturity, and support proactive public investment prioritization.

## Target Users
- Public-sector policy analysts
- Regional planning units
- Ministry and municipal strategy teams
- Infrastructure and service commissioners
- Non-technical decision-makers consuming dashboard outputs

## Public-Sector Value
- Enables earlier intervention by exposing regional service deficits before critical shortages become visible.
- Creates a unified evidence layer across social and health infrastructure domains.
- Improves transparency and auditability of planning assumptions and metrics.
- Supports defensible funding prioritization using standardized and reproducible analytics.

## Core Research Questions
1. Which regions are under-served across childcare, youth services, and hospital capacity?
2. Where is service capacity growing too slowly relative to likely regional need?
3. Which regions show persistent infrastructure deficits over time?
4. Which areas have the weakest service maturity and highest resilience risk?
5. How can historical trends be used to forecast near-term service pressure?

## Success Criteria
- Raw CSV files are ingested reproducibly and preserved unchanged.
- Data is normalized into structured analytical models with documented rules.
- KPIs are computed consistently and interpreted clearly for policymakers.
- Underserved regions are identified across domains and over time.
- Forecast outputs are transparent, explainable, and policy-relevant.
- Dashboards expose decision-ready insights and data quality status.
- Automated quality checks validate freshness, completeness, and schema consistency.

## Explicit Out-of-Scope
- Patient-level or person-level health data
- Geocoded routing and travel-time optimization
- Real-time streaming ingestion
- Causal inference claims
- Prescriptive policy recommendations beyond data-supported prioritization
- External socioeconomic enrichment unless explicitly added in a later phase
